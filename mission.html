Our Mission - Helping the Homeless in San Diego

The UCSD Houselessness Service Organization is a mutual aid group providing food, clothing, sanitary products, and resources to unhoused folks in San Diego and educating the public about issues surrounding the housing crisis. Our group student run and completely donation based!

The Homelessness Crisis in San Diego

Our organization, UCSD Houslessness Service Organization, strives to provide our houseless community, primarily in the downtown area, with essentials such as food, clothing, hygiene products, blankets, and water. We also provide resource pamphlets that contain information such as legal and housing resources. More than anything, as student activists and advocates we aim to build connections with our unhoused folk and get to know them as they get to know us as well. In terms of the logistics, our organization acquires funding by garnering donations, from booths and events that we hold on campus, and with that we buy the food and hygiene supplies, etc. that we need. Things like clothing, shoes, and blankets are usually collected through donations, and then we organize these by item type. People often just take what they need and will ask us if they are in need of something specific. Most of the time, all the warmer items are being taken right away, and in general we don’t see enough larger sizes for clothing. Finally, we have started preparing hot meals while also picking up food items from grocery stores that they don’t plan on keeping.

According to recent reports from inewsource, approximately 2,500 San Diegans are living on sidewalks and in canyons and riverbeds, which is far more than the 1,800 shelter beds available. These beds were already 97% full earlier this week, highlighting the urgent need for intervention.

The situation has worsened since the pandemic, with more than three times as many people living on downtown sidewalks than before. Unfortunately, the number of San Diegans losing housing is outpacing the number that can be helped, exacerbating the crisis.

In response to the mounting public pressure to address this issue, San Diego leaders are proposing legislation that would make it illegal to live in a tent virtually anywhere within city limits. This highlights the urgency of finding sustainable solutions to provide assistance and support for those experiencing homelessness.

Houselessness' Commitment

At Houselessness, we are deeply committed to tackling the homelessness crisis in San Diego. Our mission is to provide comprehensive support and resources to the homeless community, empowering individuals to regain stability and rebuild their lives.

Through a combination of emergency shelter services, job training programs, mental health support, and access to affordable housing, we aim to address the root causes of homelessness and help individuals reintegrate into society.

We believe that everyone deserves a safe and secure place to call home. By working together with local authorities, community partners, and compassionate volunteers like you, we strive to make a lasting impact and improve the lives of homeless individuals and families in San Diego.
